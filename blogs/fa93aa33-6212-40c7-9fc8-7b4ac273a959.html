Common Diffusion Noise Schedules and Sample Steps are Flawed

Created Date: November 12, 2023

Solves the medium brightness problem by setting SNR = 0 at final timestep T=1000

Motivation: Common schedules’ SNR does not go to 0 at final T=1000

  • This creates a gap between training and inference
  • For training, the input to the model is not completely pure noise but a small leaked signal containing the lowest frequency information, such as the overall mean of each channel.
  • As a result, the model generate samples according to the mean given at t = T, resulting in images with medium brightness
  • At inference, pure Gaussian noise is given for sampling

Solution to achieve zero terminal SNR

  • For existing non-cosine schedules, rescale existing noise schedules
  • For the cosine schedule, remove the βt clipping

To solve bad side effects of setting zero terminal SNR

  • Train with V Prediction and V Loss, which is repurposed to predict the mean of the data distribution conditioned on the prompt
  • As the terminal SNR approaches zero, classifier-free guidance becomes very sensitive and can cause images to be overexposed
  • rescale classifier-free guidance xcfg back to the original standard deviation
